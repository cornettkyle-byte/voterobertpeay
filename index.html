Personal Background

I’m Robert Peay. My beloved wife, Pat, passed away last year after a courageous battle with leukemia. Our 44 years of marriage taught me the true meaning of unconditional love. She was a renowned Rutherford County home economics educator. We raised two wonderful daughters, Dr. Rachel Peay Cornett (Lipscomb University Professor) and Katie Peay (Biosystems Engineer). I’m also the VERY proud Papa to five grandchildren: Camille, Rocco, Isaac, Charlie, and Luca. I attend North Boulevard Church with my family. I’m an alumnus of Kittrell School and MTSU. Professionally, I am retired from the manufacturing industry where I humbly labored in many roles over the years from shift work to management. I have been a small business owner and served my community as a volunteer firefighter. In my spare time I’m an outdoorsman, I farm in Readyville, and I enjoy supporting the performing arts as well as our local athletes like the Oakland Patriots.

My love for Rutherford County runs deep. My ancestors were trustees who signed the original petition to help make Rutherford County a county. Much has changed since then, but my family still resides here in District 4 and Rutherford should remain a wonderful place for everyone. From my years of experience serving as a commissioner, I know the role comes with its particular challenges and demands. My deep commitment to fighting for the best for District 4 and for all of Rutherford County has never wavered. It brings me joy to help my fellow neighbors! My servant's heart desires to continue working hard to get things right for the people of District 4.

About District 4

District 4 covers both rural and suburban areas near/on the eastern border of Rutherford County including but not limited to parts of Readyville, Milton, Lascassas, Kittrell, Bradyville, and Murfreesboro around the Oakland Schools. Goals

1. Fiscal Responsibility

My top priority is ensuring we do not overburden residents with taxes. As your Budget Chairman, I have helped guide Rutherford County into the top 1.7% of counties nationwide for financial health.

AAA Bond Rating: We are one of only 53 counties in the U.S. to maintain a AAA rating across all three agencies, saving us millions in interest.

Debt Reduction: By aggressively paying off debt early, our budget committee along with the commission has saved taxpayers $26.4 million since 2010.

Senior Advocacy: I will continue to work with our state delegation to raise the $67,000 income threshold for the property tax freeze to better protect our seniors.

2. Innovative Waste Management & Clean Water

We must reduce our dependence on landfills and protect our natural resources.

Sustainable Solutions: I am advocating for a county-led recycling center or a waste-to-energy facility to stop the practice of simply burying trash.

Protecting Infrastructure: I want to limit or stop out-of-county trash that increases our road maintenance costs.

Clean Water: I am committed to identifying and correcting the sources of pollution in our local waterways to ensure our drinking water and recreation areas remain safe.

3. Preserving Our Rural Heritage

Tennessee is losing over 200 acres of farmland every single day.

Smart Growth: I support Plan Rutherford to prevent over-development and preserve our rural farmland.

Balanced Development: We must protect our agricultural history while allowing for responsible growth only where the infrastructure and soil can support it.

4. Accessibility & Common Sense Leadership

I am one vote on the commission, but I promise to be your most vocal advocate for District 4.

Accountability: I will ensure tax dollars are spent transparently on essential needs: quality schools, public safety (fire, police, ambulance), community needs, and safe roads.

Accessible Representation: I work for you. My contact information is always public, and I encourage you to reach out at any time.

Accomplishments

I have accomplished nothing without the support of my constituents. Below you will find a list of a few highlights that I have been glad to help with over the years serving as commissioner.

Fiscal Responsibility & Leadership

  • • AAA Bond Rating: As budget chairman, I’ve helped guide our county to a rare AAA bond rating across all three agencies, helping keep our costs and debt lower.
  • • Debt Reduction: Voted to pay off our debt faster by placing half of all development tax toward debt service.
  • • Tax Relief: Supported freezing property tax increases for senior citizens and assisting our disabled, veterans, and elderly.
  • • Ethics & Governance: Served on the Rutherford County Ethics Committee since its inception.
  • • Public Voice: As chairman of Steering, I implemented the first public comment sessions before they were required by the state.

Education & School Safety

  • • SROs & Safety: Advocated for School Resource Officers (SROs) for all county schools and voted for the provision of metal detectors.
  • • Infrastructure: Advocated for recent school additions at Oakland and Riverdale and proudly support our award-winning Rutherford County Schools. Great schools attract great residents and businesses.
  • • Classroom Support: Made certain there was enough funding for textbooks for Kittrell School students.

Public Safety & Infrastructure

  • • Fire & Rescue: Fought for and established the Rutherford County Fire Rescue Department while maintaining support for our remaining volunteers.
  • • Public Safety Buildings: Worked to provide funding for public safety buildings in Lascassas and Kittrell, providing fire protection, ambulances, and health services.
  • • Highway Safety: Added sidewalks and a wider roadway to Halls Hill Pike where it was previously rated one of the most deadly roads in Rutherford County. Worked with the city and state to widen Jefferson Pike and Florence Road.
  • • Turn Lanes: Championed adding turning lanes to any future subdivision being built and added turning lanes at Highway 96, Richland Richardson Road, Compton Road, etc.
  • • Water Access: Helped get CUD water to rural areas with USDA grants and pushed to mandate that water hydrants be included in all subdivisions.

Environment & Planning

  • • Waste Management: Convinced the commission to sell the Guy James Property, which was zoned for a landfill, to MTSU and voted for the county’s own transfer station for better waste control.
  • • Sustainability: Invited to speak on a panel regarding ideas for our county at Recycle Rutherford’s public meeting.
  • • Planning Experience: Served as a Regional Planning Commission member for 8 years.

Community Preservation

  • • Kittrell Gym: Successfully navigated the return of the old Kittrell School gym to our community for its restoration and community use.
  • • Advocating for Smart Growth and Preserving Rural Farmland: Voted for Plan Rutherford which calls for less dense development in rural areas.
